Whether an individual believes business corporations make too much profit or a fair amount depends on their personal economic perspectives and priorities. Public opinion on this issue is divided, and demographic data such as gender or location cannot definitively predict a specific person's view.

**Perspectives on corporate profits include:**

*   **Business corporations make too much profit:** This viewpoint often focuses on growing wealth inequality and the gap between executive compensation and worker wages. Proponents of this view may argue that high profit margins come at the expense of consumers through higher prices or at the expense of employees through stagnant pay. They might also point to instances where corporations achieve record profits during periods of economic hardship for the general public.

*   **Most corporations make a fair and reasonable amount of profit:** This viewpoint emphasizes that profit is a necessary incentive for innovation, risk-taking, and investment. Proponents argue that profits allow companies to expand, create jobs, and provide essential goods and services. They may also note that many people benefit from corporate profits through retirement accounts, pension funds, and stock ownership, and that competitive markets naturally regulate profit levels over time.
